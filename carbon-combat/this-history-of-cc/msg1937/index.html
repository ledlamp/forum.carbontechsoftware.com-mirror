Also, editing would be great. However if you plan on making these first few journal entries the prologue you have to realize how fast you are going through the story. In just one chapter you have set up the entire backstory, while in a good amount of novels it takes the first half of the book to do so. Space it out a bit, somehow. There is a lot of potential with characters that die since anything they learn dies with them, so writing in a soldier's standpoint in either the camp raid, the retaliation mission or the attack on the R & D base could be helpful so you can get the background info in without having your main character take a full look around EVERY room he goes into, but still getting it to a decent length. In other words, take your time, even if this isn't the most fun part to write about (I know the feeling).
