Let's talk about something complete different (stick with me here).


The scientific method is scientific way to scientifically do stuff. It generally goes about as follows;
  • Come up with a hypothesis (AKA guess what will happen)
  • Do something.
  • Figure out what happened & revise your hypothesis.
For instance, when debating on how to change the shotgun, we come up with a hypothesis, do something (in this case change the shotgun) and then we figure out what happened and form a new hypothesis if needed.


Something else completely different: Consensus is when a group debates over something and comes to one solution as a group, but not necessarily individually. So if a group is debating over how to change a shotgun, then they throw out ideas until one comes up that most of the group can agree with at least partially until they figure out one that suit's the group the best.


In other words, let's get a consensus together (maybe a poll from suggested options) and vote on that for say a few days, then look at the results and see which option the group likes best. From that information we change the shotgun and if it doesn't work or something unexpected happens we do this again until it is balanced.


In other words, time to compromise for the best option you can think of cremyjr or be left out of the group.
