Pancakes,
few ideas posted on the old forum are dear to me;
2 of these would need some maps prepared ad-hoc.
I'm not sure how interested CT is in them, but i'm sure that having some support from the community (meaning you or GTR34 putting in some work) would make them all the more doable.


1st idea,
probably the most pregnant one to nurture the newbies in the CC community: enchancing the basic training that newbies are put through.
Quote from: on the old forum
http://plexpedia.com/view-12395
  • I think a map should be developed specifically for this. The map should contain a number of features, like a parcour, which you can engage on your own, OR by following the tutorial, be taken to each "obstacle" by the system, with an explanation. The "obstacles" would mirror the needs of the training steps(will become clearer in the next steps)
The training,as i envision it,  should go through steps, some about flying, some about shooting. i'd suggest starting from basic shooting ones:
the player is positioned on a platfotm.
 
  • kill a still ai car from a given position. the ai car isn't shooting back. also brief introduction on the fact that each weapon has a different firing rate, damage and reload time.
  • introduction of SHIFT to scope. possibly with an onscreen keyboard representation.
  • leading the crosshair:kill an ai car that is bouncing up and down vertically, no side movements
  • leading the crosshair:kill an ai car that is moving sideways (eft and right) on the floor respective to the view point of the player, no vertical movements
  • kill an ai car, that is doing more complex moves a bit like the ai's ingame. the ai car should be shooting back BUT with a very slow weapon, like the railgun, but having really little damage, just to add some pressure.
  • introduction of CTRL as brake, possibly with onscreen keybaord representation
  • moving on the floor: using WASD+ CTRL move on some kind of "brief race track" wihtout falling off the track, to the next point. (up to this point, all could be cleared in max 10 mins by a first timer)
  • introducing SPACEBAR to fly
  • press SPACEBAR and - when reached a target altitude between 40 and 50 meters, add CTRL to hover (with onscreen keybaord?)
  • starting from your hovering position, add shift to scope and shoot a still dartboard target placed at reasonable distance
  • introducing WASD+spacebar with onscreen keyboard. adding at the end a big fat reminder: "remember to use CTRL as brake, to slow down and stabilise yourself
  • fly through a reasonably wide/narrow, relatively short maze like system of walls. there also should be one or two points where yo need to modify your altitude(passing under and over a frontally positioned wall). Touching the walls or fallign on the floor makes you restart this.
  • introducing W+SPACEBAR+SHIFT to scope with onscreen keybaord representation, making the player try it on a brief descent to the floor with a dartboard target right in front of him.
  • the player is positioned on a platform on qhite high altitude. In front of him two massive straight walls with the space to fly in between them(let's say distant 35 meters, he's starting in the middle). On the walls hang a number of dartboard targets randomly positioned on a diagonal downwards which mirrors the loss of height while glidingwith shift pressed. The targets explode when hit. Hit all the targets on the wall while gliding downwards, to succesfully pass to the next step.
  • Picking up the health powerup: the player's health is suddenly reduced to 20%: starting from a higher platform, use SPACEBAR, WASD, and CTRL to succesfully land on a round platform with a health powerup on top of it(similar to picking the health powerups in sanctum), and avoid death.
  • The player is told that this is a final test. it will be timed and assigned a score. The setup is similar to point 14: two massive walls, distant about 40 meters, he's on a platform facing between them. The platform he starts from is on a higher altitude, the ending platform is much further away, let's say 1 km.   The targets haging on the walls should be placed not as evenly as in point 14, more like on a sinusoidal path positioned diagonally. In the last 200 meters ther should be 2 more walls added in between the bigger ones, at 20 meters distance, with targets(so he needs to fly in between a narrower space while aiming at closer targets). The player's task is to glide towards the target platfrom on the floor and while doing so, activating scope(shift) when needed to shot the targets on the walls, adding CTRL when needed to brake in order to shoot more targets when they are closer together. The player shoudl be reminded with an onscreen keyboard how to glide and and brake while gliding. on the ending platform there is an invisibility powerup- getting that one too, will result in a +30% bonus on the score of the test.
The training, being quite comprehensive, would need to have an opt-out before each new practical task, but that part is up to CT. The map to contain this could consist of really simple elements put together for the sole purpose of training, no fancy looks needed.

that stuck with me, is the gauntlet mode, particularly in it's christmass version.
On the map side of this, I'm envisioning some kind of battle island or seb's cliffhangers covered in snow;
on the game side, ideally, having "snowing" instead of "raining" as weather tickbox, a super slidy ice lake and snow high to the knees that gives away under the weight of cars and humanoids, that would  leave a short trace behind them when on the floor.

You can't transfer entire Unity 3 packages to Unity 2.

Here is what you CAN do, though:
  • Export your Unity 3 terrain as an OBJ file, and import that OBJ file into Unity 2
  • Re-import your Sketchup models into Unity 2 separatly


There MAY also be some sort of generic exporter and importer made by a third party, which runs on both Unity 2 and 3, and will therefore allow you to transfer your project if you install the exporter on both versions of Unity, but I've never actually heard of anything like that...
