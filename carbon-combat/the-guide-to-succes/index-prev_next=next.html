Hey Guys!

Sorry for having been inactive for a while.
There are varoius reasons to it, some of a practical nature(relocating to another town), and one in particular that is linked to this topic= i had been annoyed a few times too many by few users' spamming, posts with lack of content or incoherent content to the topic discussed.

I am personally of the opinion that moderating could and should be a tad more strict and some users should be banned from this forum as they are responsible for generally ruining the feeling of "maturity" the forum and it's community had before their appearance/return.

It also comes natural to me to consider that these users are generally very young, so they fall in the category of "gamers without a budget" that wouldn't buy credits anyhow, so loosing them from the forum isn't even an economical damage for CT; this kind of users can instead become damaging to CT, and so to CC, when other players that are willing to pay but expect more etiquette and maturity from the community, leave due to it, or simply don't even bothering registering, when finding a scattery unorganised, content-less, spammed out forum at their first visits.

Reporting can indeed be a helping hand, but mods can't have to run after few problematic characters all the time. It takes less time and effort for a spammer to spam then for a mod to identify, make decisions, hide, edit, correct, split topics etc.
It's abusive in the first place that any user doesn't respect the mods even so slighlty, and keeps behaving that way.

It was abusive that due to some users' abuse of the negative karma function, CT has been forced to remove it, in the same way as it woudl be abusive for somebody to abuse of the reporting function, which i guess hasnt' been done yet massively, because ti's explicit that is a highway to getting punished/banned.

In other words, dear mods, out of respect for the value of your own time and appreciation for your work,  you are not paid enough to have to keep being so gentle and invest THAT much time into keeping at bay always the same people, who happen to be also damaging for the community. Reiteration of a misbehaviour should be taken into consideration as an infraction itself.

This is why i think that beginning U2m's "When"  a very pregnant question.

I would also like to add that banning isn't a "final judgement" on a person, instead it can have an educational value, forcing those who have abused of their user freedom, to grow out of their past mistakes, for their own future's sake.

to everyone: hope you have had a nice august, i'll check back in from time to time. cyas!
