Second is the respawn time: if you were to "snipe" with the nuke then everyone would respawn by the time you get to the base, or if you fire as you come near the base you either run the risk of killing yourself or leaving yourself defenseless when everyone respawns, which remember only takes about 5 seconds. Third is the fact that it only clears near the flag and not everyone who has a line of sight on the flag or is ready to ambush you as soon ass you take the flag, for instance someone waiting at the top of the bunker in Abandoned Silos or someone on the glass bridges in Chokepoint. For sniping, remember that there would need to be something behind the target for the nuke to detonate and the target to be moving slowly enough for you to hit it with the slow moving rocket, limiting it's effectiveness at longer ranges, but remember that using it in close range is even worse for you could kill yourself, making sniping pretty much the only way to use the nuke. Imagine someone with a nuke, and someone with a plasma blaster. In addition to it's ease to avoid (due to being a slow rocket, going about the same speed as a rocket launcher rocket) it only has one shot, and if that shot misses due to say the plasma blaster car shaking the aim of the other car, the car with the nuke is completely vulnerable for a huge amount of time with no kills to show for it. If it hits, then they get the kill, but they still are vulnerable, not to mention partially blinded. Say a person is super efficient with the nuke and get not one but TWO kills every time, plus never dies, then they can get in a ten minute round a maximum of twelve kills, tops. Now compare that to your best kills stat with other weapons; significantly lower, no?


I'm going to also back this up with another fact: if you get say 10% of your screen covered in black each time you use the nuke, you have taken up half of your view by the time you finish the game, leaving you less effective in combat as you use more nukes. I was thinking the spots would cover about 15% of the screen each time you fire a nuke, so with three nukes fired you have covered a little under half your screen, and with six you are completely blind. Remember that these don't go away when you respawn, so firing a nuke is a decision you REALLY want to be sure of before you set one off. It would be limited to a role as a panic gun, a tool exclusively designed to counter high-end players or by people who just like nukes and would suffer the consequences, making them easy pickings for other players after they fire off a few.
