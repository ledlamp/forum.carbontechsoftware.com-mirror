 This  is the proposition of a “mine system”, informed by the experience with the current one and inspired  by the comments and ideas of most of the active members,including cremy’s  idea above, FCA’s comments and yesterday’s discussion with cremy and gex.
 
Let me get out of the way one  practicality:
Part of this system is based on the idea, that it would be possible to have a command to detonate mines. Specifically: the mine that amongst the ones you have deployed, is closest to your vehicle. Ideally, a right double-click, else a letter key(Z?X?C? do they already have a function?).
@CT: I’m hoping this IS actually possible, or something similar is. What WOULD be possible?
 
From the ideas cremy porposed(C3,C4,C5) originally, i’ve kept the C3 family; the C4 family’s function(being able to detonate the mines you placed) would be applied to the whole mine park, making them a more viable option vs temp invicibility. The C5 (freezing/liquid nitrogen mines) would be a viable addition in the future as an extra FAMILY(read on to know what I mean).

I’ve taken the liberty of disregarding the current mines organization, so that, instead of describing “how to change” it, I could describe few practical implications of“how would it look like at the end, when/if implemented”.
One more thing, before cutting to the bone: even tho it’s not included in the system, I’d still like to see the spiky stripes proposed by speeddemon integrated, as they have a very clear use.
 
The system’s logic is based on cross-referencing :
- 4 GENRES of mines  based on their “use”
with
-5 FAMILIES of mines  based on their “filling”
 
GENRES
a)      Ground mines- these would behave as the ones we’ve had in-shop until today. You deploy them- they stick to the floor. The biggest drawback experienced so far, is that the current mines are really easy to avoid, EVEN if you are on the floor, so all the floor mines would have the shape of the current anti-tank-bar mine; their lower practical range of use, respective to both the temp invicibility and other kinds of mines, would be offset by adding 2 more charges to the current 4, making the mine stripes as wide as a car(maybe deploying from behind the rear wheels?)and having 16 uses/life(10 at present time feels extremely limited).
b)      Bouncing Betty mines- these are still ground mines,but with a different use. When deployed, they would look  like a small lid on the floor(like the current mines look when deployed). When triggered, these mines launch into the air and then detonate at about the height of 3 meters,b becoming dangerous not only for whom triggered them, but also for  those in the surroundings.
Their lower chance of being triggered should be offset by a very high effect per single trigger, and for a total of 16 uses/life.
c)       Drop bombs- dropped from any altitude; their drop pattern  would be equal to adding G vertical downwards acceleration to the speed and direction in which the car was travelling at the precise moment of deployment.  Their scarce precision(you cant’ see underneat your car)  is offset by an unlimited number of uses, deploying 1/sec and more limited effect.
d)      Floating/contact minesFloating/contact mines- imagine a canister with 3 tiny propellers on top, keeping it floating in the air. (CC style all the way !)The dimension of the CC canister would fit the bill perfectly, these would be by far the most useable, so their number of uses max  8/life.
FAMILIES
1)      NUCLEAR FAMILY(liberator like): When triggered of detonated by user, they cause high concentrated explosion damage on a reduced radius.
2)      NITROGLYCERIN FAMILY(proximity mine like): Very easily triggered, as it is a very unstable chemical substance (as opposed to the TNT used to detonate nuclear bombs), further made more dangerous by a system of proximity sensors. It deals about half the damage then the relative nuclear version, it covers about half the radius then the relative C3 concussion version. The extremely high speed at which it incendiates the area, creates about no impact wave.
3)      The C3 CONCUSSION FAMILY: when triggered or detonated by user, they cause a very wide spherical impact wave, pushing everything away, with almost 0 explosion damage. The radius of effect depends on the family of the mine, roughly ranges between 5 meters and 40 meters(that means a sphere bigger than a red or blue base), with a gradient making the effect extremely violent if close to the blast focus, decreasing exponentially into being only a slight destabilization if at the border of the effect radius.
4)      LOCALISED EMP FAMILY: we all know what that means already; stunning the enemy J
5)      WIDER RANGE EMP FAMILY: covering about half the radius of the relative concussion family, stunning for about half the time of the relative localized family. The mine works in 2 stages,first a can of compressed highly conductive liquid(a mix of gold parcticles and chemically clean water) explodes, “spraying” everything in it’s radius. Right afterwards, the emp pulse is discharged, stunning everyone that was “sprayed” , even if it moved.
 

Please find a readable version of the system attached at the end of this post

Let me say, I have literally just put together this, so I haven’t concentrated too much on the details. Some combinations between family/genre might be OP, and might need a rethink. Some combinations have already some consideratios about leveling the mines, others are missing.. Happy to hear opinions about it, in case the community will like this, all can be further defined.

 To the game-play, this would be a big revolution, and in my opinion an improvement. It would bring some mines(that would need to be made more expensive) up to a similar usability level of the invincibility, which would still keep the edge only for being fail-safe, while mines can still be avoided by others..
 Also, for CT this means more items to sell, higher chance of people buying credits J
 
I would also like to stress that any of the “FAMILIES” could be widened in time and applied to the grenade launcher, and also the command to detonate the mines could be applied to grenades the same way..
 
FEW EXAMPLES of how the mines would become more of an integrant part of CC:
· In DM or TDM: I have equipped missile launcher and railgun, while I have a guy with shotgun right behind my tail up-close. Too close. I can’t re estabilish a good distance for my weapons, as he has the sprinter bodykit. I deploy a floating mine and detonate it straight away either damaging him badly or re estabilishing the distance or stunning him to counterattack.
· Ground mines are, simple to understand. Mostly useful for DM and TDM, same as the bouncing betties, thnks to their improved usability(more damage, more charges, more uses) one could think about placing them not only close to flags, but also close to health or invisibility pickups, with a certified harvesting result.
· Drop bombs, would be the most difficult to use effectively, but fun!  Maybe somebody would have the patience to learn to drop them in the chimneys of the blue and red bases above their own flag.
· There is one place where  no car can hit, and that is underneath it, so if anyone is attackgin you from underneath- THERE ! DROP BOMB!
· C3’s shockwave could become extremely fun and extremely useful in ctf or in any dogfight situation.

I think I need to clarify: the bouncing betty mine would use a laser trip signal pointed upwards to detect if someone is flying low above them. When the trip is hit, the mine pops up; right under your car.

It would show there is a mine in the area, but you don't know exactly where it is unless you look very closely. And that takes time.

I like drop bombs as they are not "passive" mines like the rest of them, but they need to be tweeked if they don't obtain Drop bombs could use a guiding program, always falling to the ground but slowly following the closest target. Floating mines, I think it would fit better if they were a minefield. 5 mini-mines eject out the back of your car with one use would allow them to spread over a larger area than just one big mine. The reason behind this is actually psychological; you would avoid at all costs a big floating ball that explodes, but a minefield is dangerous but possibly able to navigate if you need to. So, if you deploy a big mine behind you during an air chase the enemy's priority no. 1 would be to avoid that mine, but if it was a cloud of mines there is less intimidation and your pursuer might actually try to weave between the mini-mines. It wouldn't usually work, but it is possible, and that can be enough.
