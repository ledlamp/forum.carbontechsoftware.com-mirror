Community Outreach
posted Mid-November, 2012
I want you to look at how many people are online now, excluding bots. At best it hovers around 20 people, at worst 5. It doesn't take a genius to realize we don't appeal as much as other multiplayer games do. We don't have a big studio backing us or a energy system to do it. Some ideas I've come up with are below, if anyone has any others please post.
  • Community outreach. This is the big one; we have said we are open to new members of the community but we haven't done a good job of showing it. Since it's the only major portal to host Unity3D I'm mostly talking about Kongregate here, which we don't do a lot of good attracting the community. I can think of several reasons, including not keeping an update log off the forum, virtually no feedback on comments and a not very active developer account around the site (even before CT went AWOL). If people like our game they will stay with us and while content helps appearances matter too.
  • Controls. A newb has a horrible time controlling his car, as you have likely seen. The physics-based flying is fun but easily gets out of hand, driving has insane oversteer and weapons are hard to aim on the move. We can keep the controls but we need to make things like /flylock and hovering more noticeable, as well as add things like soft steering and aiming assist.
  • Staff. If we are to stand out from the pack we need to keep a friendly appearance, and staff (or more likely an active moderator crew) are critical. Now we can't dictate people's personal lives and I don't expect most moderators to come back but we can at least nominate a few more people. I was thinking of announcing that we are looking for moderators and seeing who comes knocking. It's pretty easy to tell who would make a decent moderator from a message, or at least who isn't.
