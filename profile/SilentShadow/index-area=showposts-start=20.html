Yes, the Shotgun is overpowered. Almost all stronger players use this gun and win easily. Basically, it all boils down to a few options that can be done to ease the pain of victims of shotguns.


1) Decrease damage by a large factor.


2) Decrease accuracy of the flecks of the shotgun blast by increasing the shot radius.


3) Decrease the reload speed, preferably by half.


4) Increase the strength of all weapons, or the defense of all armors. The most drastic and risky option, it probably shouldn't be done.

Hello,


I think a better idea would be to make the original purchase slightly cheaper. Then to make, instead of 20 cheap and un-noticeable upgrades, 4 or 5 upgrades instead.


What I mean by that, is: take a level 1 Rocket Launcher. It does a certain amount of damage, and each upgrade adds a small amount of damage to the original damage. What I think should happen is to remove so many of the upgrades, and allow the equipment to be upgraded far less times, which would make the actual upgrades far more noticeable.


So instead of having a level 20 Shotgun, you have a level 5 Shotgun instead, but they're the same thing in terms of power. What balances this is to make the upgrades a bit more expensive, but overall reducing the prices of the guns altogether if we take out 15 useless upgrades.


They should also make the default gun upgrades slightly less destructive (a level 1 Rocket Launcher could kill a Level 20 Heavy Armor in a few shots). I agree with your sentiments on the power of newbie players.


And FatCatAttack, I really would have liked to see your take on the Flamethrower, as I also think this weapon is heavily out of balance. If this game wants to keep more players for longer times at the current prices they have now, they should either balance the equipment, or add more equipment, because right now the current selection is heavily lacking!
