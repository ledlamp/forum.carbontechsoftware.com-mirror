Feedback & Ideas / New Game Design/Animations
« on: Mid-February, 2013 »
Hello Carbon Combat Community!

Before I get to my point, I just want to point out how much time I put into thinking about all of the ideas below. Please comment with your honest opinion, and say why if you like.

　
*I use the example of the rocket launcher for most of the ideas as this weapon is a base weapon of the game.*

　
ENJOY!!!

Game Design Idea #1: Reload Animation
The idea is simple. When a person is reloading their weapon, some guns will show that they are being reloaded. (e.g. Railgun: Small cartridge pops out; simple as that.) Though not all guns will be able to show some sort of Reload Animation (Flamethrower), it can be done. It can also help a player to determine what the other player is doing. Reload-wise; maybe plan out how to come at the enemy. Some weapons (Mini and Shotgun) won't need animation; they currently have an animation for ammo/reload. The other List of Reload Animation is shown below:   
     Laser Cannon: After being fired, a small amount of smoke (a smaller version of the smoke shown with a person’s low health) will spill from the front of the barrel of the cannon. As it cools down, the Laser's red color will become as it normally is or before it is fired.

      Railgun: After firing the high caliber round, the gun will cool down and a magazine/cartridge will pop out. I see the gun firing a green round instead of a blue one in my mind. The animation would possibly look better if the main color was green.

      Plasma Blaster: After the rounds of Plasma have gone, the gun will appear to "cool down" much like the Laser, but faster and with a lower amount of red light shown.  Even though there may be more, I just wanted to give a little idea of what the animation would do or look like for some if all possible weapons. I didn’t say it above, but it is possible for the Laser Cannon to have a cartridge that pops out of the cannon as well; just a little bigger and red instead of blue.
 

Game Design Idea #2: Area Effects
In this idea, different objects and parts of the environment are affected by weapons and other items. From Burn marks, to pieces of the ground falling apart, the idea would make this game that much better.

      Explosives: Every time you fire a rocket at a target, you either miss or hit it. Either way, when it hits a target or object, there is an explosion. But what happens when you look back at what your warhead hit? You see it just as it was before. When and explosive weapon, item, or object explodes, there should be a mark showing what "occurred." For instance, Lets say you shoot the ground. When the smoke clears and you can see the ground, you will see a black mark, much like what you see with most explosives.

*To add to explosives category, a new weapon. This weapon is called the Cluster Shot, or something similar to that. What it does is simple and is in the name; the explosives cluster up into one attack. Let’s say you use the Rocket Launcher. When the rocket explodes, there will be five smaller explosives thrown out about 3 feet adding an area damage or extra damage to a missed target. The strength of the Cluster is that of a missile launcher and the smaller explosives are either half or ¼ of the missile launcher’s damage. Please comment on the idea.*

·      Fire: As wee all know, there is a Flame thrower in Carbon Combat. This weapon set fire to its target (CC Combat Car). But nobody put into consideration how cool it would be to set fire to trees and other smaller parts of the ground, such as the grass on Battle Island. Now, you may be saying "That will create a major amount of lag…" You see, this statement is not technically true. You see other cars flying and moving around while they are fire. Setting fire to an object that is immobile will not really create a difference lag-wise. Setting fire to the ground would be harder to do than set a tree on fire, just so that you can control the lag that may develop. This game creates its own lag most of the time, it isn’t uncommon.   Water: Currently, no matter how you hit the water, you see a splash. Though, this splash is not very grand; even when a car flying into it at 110 miles per hour. The idea of water is to simply make splashes bigger and splash out at the angle an object entered the water. The biggest splash wouldn’t need to be any bigger than the circumference of the car itself. The same to other weapons. The laser would have no effect, while a rocket would hit the water normally and progressively splash out as the explosion does if the floor underwater is close enough.

      Dents: The idea here is very simple and will create a more realistic reaction to a weapon or object traveling and hitting another object at a high speed. The only surface/item I would like the community and myself to see for a test of the denting effect would be armor, or possibly even the vehicles. If a high caliber round from a mini gun hits a car or object, you see a bullet hole in that object. But what of the other weapons? For the Railgun, you see the same affect, but what about grenades and heavy projectiles? The only thing that happens to a car or its armor is there is an explosion; the smoke clears and what do you see? Nothing. You know they took damage, why not show they did? There is a little green bar by your health that says how much health your armor has. By doing so, the game would just be that much more amazing.   
 
Game Design Idea #3: Lighting
     Even though the lighting in Carbon Combat is amazing, there could be more additions or adaptations that could be made to make them even more remarkable.   
     Headlights: There is a setting when hosting a game where you can make it night time. But when you see what it looks like, all you really see is a dark black sky and a little difference in color on the ground. CarbonTech could make it so that everything literally looks dark, like a forest, only lighted by the moon. That is where the headlights will come in. By adding these headlights, it further shows how much better the game can look and act. It’s a car; why shouldn’t have any lights? You seen them on the car, you just don't see them.

      Explosive Light:  In the dark, or at night, is where I see this Idea much better than during the day; though it still could be possible during the day. Once an explosive weapon (e.g. Rocket Launcher) explodes, you see the explosion yes? You do not see any light around that area it exploded in. If you shot into a dark room, you couldn't see the explosion any better than you could see your own car.
　

Please, comment on what you believe about this post. Thanks!
