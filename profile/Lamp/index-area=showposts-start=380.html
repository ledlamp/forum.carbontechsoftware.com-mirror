The second thing you need is good screen capture software.
For Mac users:
  • Built-in screen capture tool. Activate with shift + command + 3 for entire screen, and shift + command + 4 for selection. The problem is that shift will open scope.
  • Snapz Pro X. (Not free but gives a trial) This tool will freeze the screen and is great for perfectly timed pics! It also records video.
  • Jing. Great for quick pics or vids. Includes annotation tool and quick upload to Screencast.com.

For Windows users:
  • Print screen & Paint. Although it only allows you to take one screenshot, and will be lost if you copy something else. However it is good for capturing one perfectly timed photo with a hit of a button.
  • Jing. Great for quick pics or vids. Includes annotation tool and quick upload to Screencast.com.
  • Greenshot. Dunno anything bout it.
