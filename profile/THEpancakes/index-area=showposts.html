Carbon Combat / Re: Sketchup question

It's a very annoying and very messy task, but it is possible. Here's how:

  • At the end of your track, where you want the corkscrew to start, extrude 24 small segments, like this:
  • One by one, start grabbing each of the lines along the extruded path, and rotate them a little bit every time. You may want to turn on X-Ray view for this.
  • Once you've done this for a while, you should have something that looks like this:
  • Keep going like that until you have a 360 degree turn, then use the soften edges tool to smooth everything out.

Carbon Combat / Re: New environments for CC
« on: Mid-July, 2012 »
You can't transfer entire Unity 3 packages to Unity 2.

Here is what you CAN do, though:
  • Export your Unity 3 terrain as an OBJ file, and import that OBJ file into Unity 2
  • Re-import your Sketchup models into Unity 2 separatly


There MAY also be some sort of generic exporter and importer made by a third party, which runs on both Unity 2 and 3, and will therefore allow you to transfer your project if you install the exporter on both versions of Unity, but I've never actually heard of anything like that...
