Carbon Combat / Re: Sketchup question
« on: Late July, 2012 »
Sorry to be in disarray with the 3d models above, but i  must say that with real life physics none of the proposed models would work=> the car would fall nomatter the momentum.

Look at the image below:


To hope for the car to get through the loop , you need to approach it frontally, aligned to the "track".

The moment of vertical suspension is kept to a minimum, not to loose too much grip and to produce the top centrifugal force(depends on the radius of the loop in that fraction and the speed of the car).

m=mass
r= radius of the loop if it were a perfect circle
v= speed of the car at the top of the loop(when upside down)

the centrifugal force= m v2/r
the gravitational force=mg
centrifugal force needs to be equal to or bigger than the gravitational force
few lines of basic math to get the relation v2=gr    => v= (square root of gr)

you can easilly calcualte, based on the speed a cc car can keep on the track, the max radius of the loop you can build and expect the car to keep. Then just tighten a bit the top to create an increase in centrifugal foce just when needed and you should get a success.

You could place more loops one after the other, sideways, provided they are driveable enough to keep the speed of the car almost constant.


hope this helps!
