ORIGINAL POST:

Hello,


Recently, there has been a complaint about people moderating the chat room on the forum ineffectively, or simply not moderating at all. I decided that a simple solution to this would be to create an IRC chat bot, upload it to the channel that the Forum Chat room is on, and allow it to do the moderating, rather than having an actual person do it. This one main drawback to this method is the fact that it would have to be placed on a server with high uptime- if the server crashes, the IRC Chat Bot does not work during server downtime.


An IRC Chat Bot could provide multiple functions to the Forum Chat:
Commands: Commands such as /ban, /dateandtime, /evict, /members, etc. All of these commands would be relayed to the chat bot, the bot would interpret these commands, and would perform an intended function. Commands could vary based on the need for different functions.
Relaying Chat Messages: The IRC Chat Bot could relay messages with certain words or phrases to the PM of a moderator, so that they could deal with the problem appropriately. As well, it could also ban users with a matching IP of users who used certain words and phrases that were insulting or explicit.
Moderator Reporting System: Rather than sending screenshots of something someone said in a post, why not have the IRC bot send it automatically? By using a command, such as /sendtomod, it would record the conversation, and would send the information to a moderator, along with recording the IP addresses of people involved (IP address recording is optional).


Please, feel free to present ideas and opinions on this idea- if reaction seems positive, I will create this IRC bot when I next have a little bit of free time.

Feedback & Ideas / Re: Transferring credits
« on: Mid-July, 2012 »
Cremyjr,


Overall, I believe that your idea was posted with the best possible intent, however, there is one vital part of this idea that I can see, which (sadly) will probably never make it possible in-game: Competitive Marketing. In essence, competitive marketing is where a person or business finds another person or business selling or providing the same service to consumers. They then make their prices LOWER than the other person or business to attract more customers to their product or service. The same could be applied here...


Let's say that someone (who plays a lot of Carbon Combat) has a lot of credits. He then looks at the prices to buy normal credits. With this knowledge, he then sets up a separate website where people can buy Carbon Combat credits... for half the price that CarbonTech sells them for in Carbon Combat! Even though Carbon Combat is a free game, CarbonTech still needs some way to support himself, and this is partially done through selling credits. If no more people buy credits from CarbonTech, how will Carbon Combat pay for its servers? As you can see, this principle will make it impossible to allow players to transfer credits from one account to another.

Feedback & Ideas / Re: Ideas and Suggestions
« on: Mid-July, 2012 »

Carbon Combat Community,

I have an idea (yes, I know that I am replying to my own post): Carbon Fibre Reactive Body Armor. This armor would be sold exclusively for the Android, and would look like advanced, futuristic body armor (image here) for the android. It would have the same Carbon Fibre Texture as used in the Carbon Fibre Body Kit. The armor would have two main functions:
  • The armor would absorb 50% of weapons damage, resulting in any weapons hitting the android would only deal half of their normal damage. Because an android is twice as vulnerable to weapons as a vehicle is, with this armor, weapons would have proportionally equal damage on an android, as an unarmored car (sorry if that is confusing).
  • This 50% of damage that is absorbed builds up a "charge" in the body armor. When the body armor is fully charged, it has the capacity to release an electrical discharge toward a vehicle or android within range. This electrical discharge would deal 6/10 damage to other entities in-game.
Some proposed statistics:
Armor Statistics:
Protection*: 50%
Electrical Discharge Statistics:
Clip Size: 1
Fire Rate: Varies Depending on Energy Buildup
Accuracy: 9/10
Damage: 6/10
Area Damage: 1/10
Automatic**: Yes
Cost: 8,000 - 10,000 credits


*An android has half the health of a car. Because this armor absorbs 50% of weapons damage, a weapon would take away the same amount of a health bar as it would on an unarmored car. Let me provide an example to make it more clear:
If an unarmored car gets hit with a railgun, it loses 90% of its health bar. If an unarmored android gets hit with a railgun, it dies (because the android has half the health of a car). Now, if an android equipped with the Carbon Fibre Reactive Body Armor is hit with a railgun, it will only lose 90% of its health bar, just like an unarmored car.

**The electricity would discharge toward a car automatically, whenever a car is within range. The player does not get to choose the time that the electricity discharges toward another car.

Please tell me what you think of this idea.
