Feedback & Ideas / Re: game mode ideas
« on: Late October, 2012 »
Ha! Game mode ideas. I love this. Let me shell out some:


The generics: Common game modes in multiplayer games. You know the majority, KOTH, reverse tag (juggernaut), various CTF modifications, "hold on to the pickup", elimination, as well as others you might know from other games. They would all be great to have a CC spin on them.


Chase: Unique gamemode idea that could fit into CC. One player is unarmed/can't fire while every other player is out to kill the unarmed player. Lots of variations, such as score being calculated in time chased or how many times you kill the unarmed player, and whether the players who gets the kill becomes the new unarmed player on the spot or everyone gets a turn. To prevent flying eternally upward flying is disabled for the unarmed player, and the unarmed player can't bail.


Official Race: Get through the checkpoints before your opponents/opposing team. Weapons could or could not be disabled. Why do we not have this yet?


Equipment Grants: Game type that has all players assumes the equipment loadout of the match's host. Sneak ninja invisibility matches, sniper duels, melee n' hooks, or whatever you think of, assuming the host has bought the equipment beforehand. No freebies, one gamemode for that is good enough.


In short, I approve ANY new game type assuming it doesn't break the game somehow.

Feedback & Ideas / Re: Samurai Regen (overlook)
« on: Late October, 2012 »
It would be awesome in survival mode. My idea to make it useful:
  • Give insane protection, around 90%
  • Make it really fragile and easy to degrade
It would be a hit n' run armor like the ninja regen, but operates differently. It wouldn't be as good as say heavy armor in long-term engagements but it would be able to compete with certain weapons. That 90% is subtracted from the first hit and although damage degrades the armor instead of the # of hits, the fact that the first hit is degraded means snipers and other slow-firing weapons would be less effective. It would also mean that with two shotguns, the samurai armor wins if both players hit, so to counter you would need to be a bit tactical by whittling down the armor protection first, then going for the health hit quickly. Plus it would be just fun to use, in my opinion.

Feedback & Ideas / Re: Different types of bots (modify)
« on: Late October, 2012 »
This is...interesting. I like the idea of being able to choose your killstreak, and it would be interesting to see how it plays out. I'm really liking it for several reasons:
  • Feels like you can get your own "personal" favorite bot, if they are balanced correctly. More customization without any increase in power, yet you are more effective due to your killstreak fitting your playstyle. For instance I would love those repair bots myself being a heavy armor user and not a ninja regener.
  • Rewards players with more skill. This is because say I like repair bots, but if I'm not smart I call them while I have full health, or if I level up my bots and am unable to cash in on the double killstreak (note on double killstreaks below).
  • Counterable. If an enemy player gets a shield bot, you can either be an idiot and try to shoot the car or you can blow the shield first and shoot the bots. Once the bots are dead, the players are on even ground, but it takes a smart and skilled player to know to shoot the small bots.
  • Anti-bot weapons becomes better. The shotgun is okay against bots but they can be just too mobile to deal with the shotgun. You want a weapon that can shoot them at a distance and reloads fast enough to deal with multiple bots, with damage not being as much a factor. That means plasma blaster, minigun, and maybe missile launcher if the player is good with it, weapons that right now don't see a lot of use with the decline of sniper rifles.
I think there should be just one change, however. The killstreak and double killstreak requirements need to come down. 6 is already a pretty large amount of kills for one life, and you want to try for 12? That's only going to happen in super easy rooms, and would encourage farming or just not be used at all for the majority of the time.


Otherwise, cool idea. I never saw this before, I think. I'll check out the original thread now.
