I think I need to clarify: the bouncing betty mine would use a laser trip signal pointed upwards to detect if someone is flying low above them. When the trip is hit, the mine pops up; right under your car.

It is a bit more powerful that your suggestions, but I think you are low-balling the mines. In addition of the laser trip wire to my mine, how about burying the majority of the ground mines under ground and only showing the trip, maybe as a high-tech trip in CC theme that gives off a light based on team? It would show there is a mine in the area, but you don't know exactly where it is unless you look very closely. And that takes time.

I like drop bombs as they are not "passive" mines like the rest of them, but they need to be tweeked if they don't obtain Drop bombs could use a guiding program, always falling to the ground but slowly following the closest target. Otherwise with the speed of cars and the fact that you need to get above the target (most players spend their days more in the air than the ground, as stated before) this would be a waste.

Floating mines, I think it would fit better if they were a minefield. 5 mini-mines eject out the back of your car with one use would allow them to spread over a larger area than just one big mine. The reason behind this is actually psychological; you would avoid at all costs a big floating ball that explodes, but a minefield is dangerous but possibly able to navigate if you need to. So, if you deploy a big mine behind you during an air chase the enemy's priority no. 1 would be to avoid that mine, but if it was a cloud of mines there is less intimidation and your pursuer might actually try to weave between the mini-mines. It wouldn't usually work, but it is possible, and that can be enough.
