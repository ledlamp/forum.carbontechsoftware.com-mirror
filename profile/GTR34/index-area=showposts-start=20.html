I'll take this sentence-by-sentence.


1: Complain to Unity. I don't think that CC can efficiently run 40 bajillion particle effects just to look shiny.


2: That is with the world creation. When creating worlds, you can apply textures to a terrain, but unfortunately there is no way to do it perfectly. Good texturers can make it look nice, but you still can't get much variety without it looking ridiculous.


3: The shininess probably has to do with lightmapping and the light sources. In the Unity creator, you can adjust the intensity of a world, making it really dark and gloomy, or you can make it extremely bright and spazzed.


I don't know if you know that or not, but I thought I'd bring it up anyway.


Yoshi,


Textures can be applied to look nice, trust me. There are methods.


2. The shininess can be fixed. Cars can be UV mapped and textured AND lightmapped AND even have an alpha channel for various shininess. Trust me, I've done it. So the car would look like it has depth and not just super shine. (Specular reflections, and light cubemap reflections)


3. The car is reflecting a cubemap. The cubemap looks nothing like the terrain (looks like the car is in studio)


The thing is, If CC is aiming for cartoon styling, there are cartoon shaders that can put hard edges on objects and make the world look cartoon-ish with a very cool style. If he's going for realism, I know how to design car textures and help improve all of the world textures and shadows to look more photo real. The problem is, It looks like it's stuck halfway in between.
