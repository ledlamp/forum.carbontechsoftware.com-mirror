In every racing game that you have to pay for visual upgrades I only buy them at the end, instead buying performance upgrades)

The sort of spoiler I would suggest would be only slightly angled at around 10' and have two blades, held up by narrow fin like piece. With the sides of the spoilers, it is waaaay too big, I suggest redesigning it so there are two pairs of side bits attached to each blade/spoiler of a medium size.

This of course would still be hanging of the edge of the car because of the shape of the rear end, this is one of the main reasons a really big spoiler wouldn't fit in, the back of the car is high enough as it is, adding a big spoiler would stick out completely.

I am not too sure about adding a spoiler at the back as these sort of cars don't have spoilers, for good reasons, if you were to re-design it so the back end is lower, a spoiler would fit in a lot nicer. As it is a high back end is not common for these types of cars.

If you don't want to redesign it that much or don't think you should then I suggest paying more attention to the sideskirts, air intakes and exausts coming out of the side would be really cool. The back end of the side skirt looks a bit too low.

Lastly the rear bumper area, going with the style of car you've chosen I suggest having fins like this: http://www.revozport.com/webpics/FERRARI/F430/Diffuser%20Fins/F430_press-diffuser.jpg
but more angled and exaggerated.
