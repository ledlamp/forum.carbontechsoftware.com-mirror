As much as this is an argument about to happen...that really is not the whole picture. It would be great to have lots of people, but the chat needs to be able to function (as in less nonsense, more conversation). My logic is this:

IF Amount of Moderation = Amount of People * Rowdiness, THEN Chat is good.

IF Amount of Moderation < Amount of People * Rowdiness, THEN Chat is chaotic.

IF Amount of Moderation > Amount of People * Rowdiness, THEN Chat is too strict.

The value of moderation can change with the person viewing the chat, as well as the rowdiness. Currently the chat has no moderation or if the mod powers are given out wrongly even negative moderation, making chat more chaotic. We don't have a lot of people though, but there are a lot of cool features that do distract from the fact that it is a chat and make it more rowdy. So if moderators apply enough force to keep the amount of people and rowdiness in check without being too strict, then we have the ideal chat, getting better the higher amount of values are on all sides. Of course, since how much moderation is too much/little moderation and the general level of rowdiness are based on personal opinion, some people might have different views of the ideal chat. That said, if the majority of people think chat is too chaotic then we need to balance out the formula by either adding more moderation, lowering the amount of people (last resort tactic) and the rowdiness of the chat room.

TL;DR: Basically, we want to increase moderation or lower the rowdiness of the room before lowering the amount of people, since the less amount of people in chat the less fun (as a general rule).
